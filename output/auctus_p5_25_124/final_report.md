# Audit Report

**Total findings:** 5

## Critical (3)

### F-001: Unverified ACO tokens let attackers sweep arbitrary writer-held ERC20 balances

**Confidence:** high | **Locations:** `onchain_auto/0xe7597f774fd0a15a617894dc39d45a28b97afa4f/Contract.sol:1, onchain_auto/src/FlawVerifier.sol:230, onchain_auto/src/FlawVerifier.sol:271`

`write()` trusts the caller-supplied `acoToken` for `collateral()`, `strikeAsset()`, and mint behavior. A malicious token can make `transferFrom`/`mintTo` succeed without moving real collateral, then `_sellACOTokens()` uses the untrusted `strikeAsset()` value and transfers the writer's entire balance of that token to the caller.

**Impact:** Any ERC20 balance already resident in the writer, including WETH or stablecoins, can be permissionlessly drained without supplying real collateral or selling real option tokens.

**Paths:**

- Deploy a fake token that implements the expected interface, returns a malicious collateral token that always reports successful `transferFrom`, and reports the target asset from `strikeAsset()`

- Call `write(fakeToken, 1, attackerEOA, "")` while sending the minimum ETH required by `write()`

- `_sellACOTokens()` succeeds, then transfers the writer's full balance of the chosen strike asset to the attacker

*Round 1 | Agents: codex_1, opencode_1*

### F-002: Caller-chosen exchange can steal the writer's entire ETH balance

**Confidence:** high | **Locations:** `onchain_auto/0xe7597f774fd0a15a617894dc39d45a28b97afa4f/Contract.sol:1`

The caller fully controls `exchangeAddress` via `setExchange(exchangeAddress)`, and `_sellACOTokens()` blindly forwards `address(this).balance` to `_exchange.call(...)` before any accounting. There is no validation that the target is a trusted exchange or that the ETH returned corresponds to the current trade.

**Impact:** Any ETH sitting in the writer, including prior premiums or accidentally stranded funds, can be sent to an attacker-controlled EOA or contract and never returned.

**Paths:**

- Wait until the writer holds ETH

- Call `write(...)` with `exchangeAddress` set to an attacker-controlled EOA or contract and benign calldata

- The low-level call transfers the writer's full ETH balance to the attacker-controlled destination, and the rest of the function cannot recover it

*Round 1 | Agents: codex_1*

### F-003: ETH-collateral writes can be underfunded with protocol-owned ETH

**Confidence:** high | **Locations:** `onchain_auto/0xe7597f774fd0a15a617894dc39d45a28b97afa4f/Contract.sol:1`

For ETH-collateral series, `write()` only checks `msg.value > 0` and then calls `mintToPayable{value: collateralAmount}(msg.sender)`. If `collateralAmount` exceeds `msg.value`, the shortfall is silently funded from the writer's pre-existing ETH balance.

**Impact:** Attackers can mint options collateralized by the writer's native ETH instead of their own, externalizing collateral losses to the protocol or prior users.

**Paths:**

- Wait until the writer holds ETH

- Call `write(validEthCollateralToken, largeCollateralAmount, exchangeAddress, exchangeData)` while sending only 1 wei

- `mintToPayable{value: collateralAmount}` consumes the writer's ETH balance to collateralize options minted to the attacker

*Round 1 | Agents: codex_1*

## High (2)

### F-004: Premium settlement pays out whole-contract balances instead of the current trade delta

**Confidence:** high | **Locations:** `onchain_auto/0xe7597f774fd0a15a617894dc39d45a28b97afa4f/Contract.sol:1`

After the exchange call, `_sellACOTokens()` transfers `_balanceOfERC20(token, address(this))` for ERC20 strikes or `address(this).balance` for ETH, rather than the incremental proceeds generated by the current write.

**Impact:** Residual strike assets or ETH from earlier users, failed fills, partial settlements, or unsolicited transfers can be permissionlessly claimed by the next caller whose trade reaches the same payout branch.

**Paths:**

- A previous interaction or direct transfer leaves strike tokens or ETH inside the writer

- A later caller invokes `write()` for a series whose strike asset matches that balance, or uses an ETH-settling path

- The contract forwards the entire leftover balance to the later caller as if it were their premium

*Round 1 | Agents: codex_1*

### F-005: Any WETH balance can permanently brick ETH-strike writes

**Confidence:** high | **Locations:** `onchain_auto/0xe7597f774fd0a15a617894dc39d45a28b97afa4f/Contract.sol:1, onchain_auto/src/FlawVerifier.sol:240, onchain_auto/src/FlawVerifier.sol:255`

For ETH-strike options, `_sellACOTokens()` calls `IWETH(weth).withdraw(wethBalance)` when the writer holds any WETH. The resulting ETH is sent from the WETH contract, but `receive()` reverts unless `msg.sender == _exchange`, so the withdraw path always reverts when WETH is present.

**Impact:** A 1 wei WETH transfer is enough to poison the contract and make ETH-strike writes revert until the WETH balance is somehow removed, but the contract exposes no recovery path to do so.

**Paths:**

- Transfer any amount of WETH directly to the writer

- Call `write()` for an ETH-strike ACO token

- `_sellACOTokens()` attempts `WETH.withdraw`, the callback hits `receive()`, and the transaction reverts because the sender is WETH rather than `_exchange`

*Round 1 | Agents: codex_1, opencode_1*
