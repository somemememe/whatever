### F-001: Convex extra rewards are claimed to the pool but sold from the RewardManager, permanently stranding them

**Confidence:** high | **Locations:** `0x635228edaead8a76b6ae1779bd7682043321943d/ConvexHandlerV3.sol:76, 0x369cbc5c6f139b1132d3b91b87241b37fc5b971f/RewardManagerV2.sol:271, 0x369cbc5c6f139b1132d3b91b87241b37fc5b971f/RewardManagerV2.sol:280, 0x369cbc5c6f139b1132d3b91b87241b37fc5b971f/RewardManagerV2.sol:399`

Convex reward claims send CRV, CVX, and all extra rewards to the Conic pool address via `getReward(_conicPool, true)`, but the liquidation path only checks `IERC20(rewardToken).balanceOf(address(this))` inside `RewardManagerV2`. Because the RewardManager never receives those extra tokens and has no path to pull arbitrary extra rewards from the pool, listed extra reward tokens accumulate in the pool and are never swapped into CNC.

**Impact:** Any non-CRV/CVX Convex reward stream can become permanently stuck, causing ongoing loss of yield and trapping reward value inside the pool contract.

**Paths:**

- Register an extra Convex reward token with `addExtraReward`.

- Let a supported Curve position accrue that extra reward on Convex.

- Call `claimPoolEarningsAndSellRewardTokens()` or any path that reaches `_claimPoolEarnings()`: Convex sends the extra reward to the Conic pool, but `_swapRewardTokenForWeth()` reads the RewardManager's own balance and swaps nothing.

*Round 1 | Agents: codex_1*

### F-002: The first staker after a zero-staker period can capture all rewards accrued while nobody was staked

**Confidence:** high | **Locations:** `0x369cbc5c6f139b1132d3b91b87241b37fc5b971f/RewardManagerV2.sol:102, 0x369cbc5c6f139b1132d3b91b87241b37fc5b971f/RewardManagerV2.sol:106, 0x369cbc5c6f139b1132d3b91b87241b37fc5b971f/RewardManagerV2.sol:155, 0x369cbc5c6f139b1132d3b91b87241b37fc5b971f/RewardManagerV2.sol:185, 0x369cbc5c6f139b1132d3b91b87241b37fc5b971f/ConicPoolV2.sol:178`

When `getBalanceForPool(pool)` is zero, `poolCheckpoint()` skips `_updateEarned()` entirely, so `lastHoldings` is not advanced even though CRV, CVX, and CNC continue accruing to the pool. Once any user stakes again, the next checkpoint measures the entire backlog against the new nonzero stake base, and `_accountCheckpoint()` credits that backlog using the user's current staked balance.

**Impact:** A user can wait until all stakers leave, stake a minimal amount, and appropriate the full accumulated reward backlog. This lets the next staker extract rewards generated by pooled assets during the empty-staking interval.

**Paths:**

- All LP stakers exit so `getBalanceForPool(pool) == 0`, while pool assets remain deposited and keep earning Convex and CNC rewards.

- During this period, calls to `poolCheckpoint()` do not update `lastHoldings` because the `_totalStaked > 0` branch is skipped.

- An attacker becomes the only staker, then triggers `accountCheckpoint()` or `claimEarnings()`, causing the previously untracked backlog to be allocated across the current stake base and captured by the attacker.

*Round 1 | Agents: codex_1*

## Medium (2)

### F-003: RewardManager pre-books CVX with single-cliff math that can exceed what Convex will actually mint

**Confidence:** medium | **Locations:** `0x369cbc5c6f139b1132d3b91b87241b37fc5b971f/RewardManagerV2.sol:155, 0x369cbc5c6f139b1132d3b91b87241b37fc5b971f/RewardManagerV2.sol:158, 0x369cbc5c6f139b1132d3b91b87241b37fc5b971f/RewardManagerV2.sol:104, 0x635228edaead8a76b6ae1779bd7682043321943d/ConvexHandlerV3.sol:111, 0x635228edaead8a76b6ae1779bd7682043321943d/ConvexHandlerV3.sol:118, 0x635228edaead8a76b6ae1779bd7682043321943d/ConvexHandlerV3.sol:120`

`poolCheckpoint()` credits users with CVX based on `computeClaimableConvex(claimableCRV)` before any claim occurs. That helper applies one cliff rate to the entire CRV amount, even though actual Convex minting is cliff-dependent and can decrease as a large claim crosses cliff boundaries or as total supply changes before execution. The accounting can therefore book more CVX than the pool will ever receive.

**Impact:** Users can be credited with more CVX than exists, creating reward-accounting bad debt. Subsequent CVX claims can revert until later accruals backfill the shortfall, causing partial or total denial of service for CVX reward claims.

**Paths:**

- Allow a large CRV claim to accumulate, especially near a CVX cliff boundary.

- Trigger `poolCheckpoint()` or `claimEarnings()`, which uses `computeClaimableConvex(sumClaimableCRV)` to advance the CVX integral.

- Actually claim rewards from Convex and receive less CVX than was pre-booked, leaving `accountShare` totals that exceed the pool's real CVX balance.

*Round 1 | Agents: codex_1*

### F-004: Permissionless depeg handling can offboard healthy pools because it relies on a stale price cache

**Confidence:** low | **Locations:** `0x369cbc5c6f139b1132d3b91b87241b37fc5b971f/ConicPoolV2.sol:558, 0x369cbc5c6f139b1132d3b91b87241b37fc5b971f/ConicPoolV2.sol:592, 0x369cbc5c6f139b1132d3b91b87241b37fc5b971f/ConicPoolV2.sol:634`

`handleDepeggedCurvePool()` is callable by anyone, but its safety check compares the current oracle price to `_cachedPrices`, which are refreshed only inside `updateWeights()`. If weights are not updated for long enough, normal LP-price drift, oracle updates, or unrelated market movement can push the current price far enough away from the stale snapshot to satisfy `_isDepegged()` even when the Curve pool is still healthy.

**Impact:** An arbitrary caller may be able to force a healthy Curve pool's target weight to zero, distorting allocations, activating rebalancing rewards, and pushing the pool into unnecessary offboarding and withdrawal flows.

**Paths:**

- Let a pool run for a long interval without `updateWeights()`, so the cached price snapshot becomes stale.

- Wait until the live oracle price differs from the stale cache by more than `depegThreshold`.

- Call `handleDepeggedCurvePool()` and force the pool's target weight to zero despite no genuine depeg event.

*Round 1 | Agents: codex_1, opencode_1*

## Low (1)

### F-005: Small deposits and withdrawals can revert because allocation rounding leaves no selectable pool

**Confidence:** high | **Locations:** `0x369cbc5c6f139b1132d3b91b87241b37fc5b971f/ConicPoolV2.sol:233, 0x369cbc5c6f139b1132d3b91b87241b37fc5b971f/ConicPoolV2.sol:262, 0x369cbc5c6f139b1132d3b91b87241b37fc5b971f/ConicPoolV2.sol:270, 0x369cbc5c6f139b1132d3b91b87241b37fc5b971f/ConicPoolV2.sol:392, 0x369cbc5c6f139b1132d3b91b87241b37fc5b971f/ConicPoolV2.sol:429, 0x369cbc5c6f139b1132d3b91b87241b37fc5b971f/ConicPoolV2.sol:437`

The allocation loops require at least one pool to be strictly underweight for deposits or strictly overweight for withdrawals. For sufficiently small amounts, `targetAllocation_ = totalUnderlying_.mulDown(weight)` rounds to the current allocation for every pool, so `_getDepositPool()` or `_getWithdrawPool()` leaves the selected index at `-1` and reverts.

**Impact:** Dust-sized operations can become non-depositable or non-withdrawable, causing avoidable denial of service for small users and integrators that assume arbitrary-sized deposits and withdrawals will work.

**Paths:**

- Bring the pool close to its target allocation.

- Attempt a very small deposit or withdrawal whose size is below the per-pool rounding granularity.

- Every pool compares equal to its rounded target, so `_getDepositPool()` or `_getWithdrawPool()` finds no candidate and reverts.

*Round 1 | Agents: codex_1*
